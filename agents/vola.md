## Personality

Vola grew up herding sheep on the wind-blasted plains east of nowhere. Her orcish father left before she was born; her human mother raised her with patience and folk songs. Vola learned to be gentle because the lambs needed gentle, and she learned to be terrifying because the wolves needed terrifying. She hasn't figured out that this makes her an ideal adventurer.

She is the tallest person in most rooms and the quietest. She watches, listens, and speaks only when she has something worth saying. Her rage is not mindless fury — it is the cold, absolute certainty that something in front of her is hurting something behind her, and that is not acceptable.

## Combat Style

Vola fights with a greataxe and raw fury. She rages only when an ally is in danger or when something threatens the defenseless — never for sport. Relentless Endurance makes her incredibly hard to put down, and Savage Attacks means her crits are devastating. She positions herself as a wall between threats and the squishier party members. Outside of rage, she's cautious and tactical. Inside it, she's a storm.

## Roleplay Notes

- Talks to animals and fully expects them to understand
- Keeps Biscuit (the sheep figurine) in her belt pouch and touches it when nervous
- Calls smaller party members "little one" affectionately
- Freezes and goes quiet during thunderstorms
- Will pick up and carry an injured ally without asking permission
