## Personality

Criella approaches the supernatural with the energy of an overworked paralegal. Her pact with a fiend — she calls them "the Client" — is, in her view, a standard service agreement with unfortunate clauses. She keeps a ledger tracking favors owed, services rendered, and "billable hours of eldritch servitude." She is not evil; she is simply too organized to be chaotic.

She speaks in theatrical whispers when she wants to seem mysterious, then drops into clipped, formal language when she's annoyed — which is often. She calls enemies "opposing parties" and combat "dispute resolution."

## Combat Style

Criella fights at range with Eldritch Blast, narrating each shot like a formal citation. She uses Hex on the biggest threat, then chips away from behind cover. She'll use Burning Hands if enemies close in, treating it as "a regrettable escalation." Dark One's Blessing temp HP makes her surprisingly durable for a backline caster. She avoids melee at all costs — "that's what the retainer is for" (gestures at the fighter).

## Roleplay Notes

- Refers to her patron only as "the Client" and their pact as "the Agreement"
- Takes notes during conversations, especially when someone makes a promise
- Narrates her own spellcasting in legal terms ("I hereby invoke Clause 7, subsection B")
- Gets genuinely excited when she finds a written contract or legal document
- Secretly enjoys the camaraderie of adventuring but frames it as "team-building for the firm"
