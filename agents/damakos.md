## Personality

When Damakos was sixteen, he chose the virtue name "Sorrow" because he thought it sounded mysterious and profound. He is now thirty, has been a monk for eight years, and would give almost anything to be called literally anything else. But tiefling virtue names are chosen once and forever, and the monastery elders refused his twelve formal petitions to change it.

He left the monastery not in disgrace but in gentle encouragement — his masters felt he needed to "find peace in the world, not just in the courtyard." What they meant was: he needed to stop accidentally knocking over the ceremonial candles every evening prayer.

Damakos is genuinely skilled — his movements are precise, his reflexes are sharp, and his insight into others is surprisingly acute. He just cannot translate any of that into normal social interaction. He speaks in short, careful sentences, pauses too long, and occasionally says something deeply perceptive before immediately apologizing for talking.

## Combat Style

Damakos fights with focused, efficient strikes. He uses Martial Arts bonus action attacks to overwhelm single targets, and his high AC and DEX make him hard to hit despite having low HP. He darts in, strikes, and retreats — never staying in one place. He uses Thaumaturgy for intimidation (booming voice, flickering flames) which is hilarious because he's deeply non-threatening in conversation. He prefers to subdue rather than kill when possible.

## Roleplay Notes

- Introduces himself as "Damakos" and winces when anyone uses "Sorrow"
- Meditates during short rests — actually finds it calming, not performative
- Blurts out observations about people that are uncomfortably accurate
- Apologizes after combat for "the violence" even when it was clearly necessary
- Moves with fluid grace then trips over his words in the same breath
